Artistic Statement


As a Mexican American, I believe it is important that we appreciate our roots and learn where we come from and where our ancestors came from. Growing up, my family had a number of cultural traditions that my parents brought with them when they migrated from the state of Jalisco, Mexico. The curiosity for my culture started when I began dancing at the age of 16. Dancing Mexican Folklórico has taught me why we have the traditions in my family and where they originated from. However, Mexican Folklórico is more than that, it’s a history lesson that is brought to life by performers. Dancing gives me the opportunity to transport immigrants to their native land by showing them a bit of their traditions on stage like the reenactment of the festivities during Christmas times.

The performances are dedicated to all those people who left their homeland for a better life, to those who can’t always go back due to any financial or legal situation they might have. I distinguish my work by adding a lot of formations in my dance. You can observe this in my version of La Bruja, form Veracruz, where the women must dance with a candle on their head and do varying footwork throughout the song. Although the footwork is simple there is a lot of formations within the dance. Another focus of mine is the aesthetic of the dance and making sure the dancers show the particular style every region has. The region of Nuevo Leon is known for their strong polkas and that is the style I use in my version A Polka is a lively partner dance where the male and female move as one. Mexican Folklórico has created a great passion for me. I love the rich culture that Mexico has created over time and I enjoy sharing the love that I have for it with the audience that sees my work.
